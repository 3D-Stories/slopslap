## Sentence-pattern before → after (verbatim, marketing register)

**1. "Not just X, but Y" → synthetic cadence**
- Bad: "Not just a CRM, but a revenue engine."
- Good: "Closes deals in half the steps."

**2. "More than X" framing → synthetic cadence / emptiness**
- Bad: "More than a platform, it's a growth partner."
- Good: "Runs your outreach, tracks your pipeline, and flags deals going cold."

**3. Defining by negation → synthetic cadence**
- Bad: "This isn't your typical agency."
- Good: "We build paid campaigns, run them, and hand you the playbook when we leave."

**4. Self-congratulatory claim without proof → simulation (unsupported claim)**
- Bad: "Trusted by thousands of businesses." / "Industry-leading support."
- Good: "Used by 4,200 teams across 30 countries." / "Median response time: 4 minutes."

**5. Aspirational filler → emptiness (delete)**
- Bad: "We believe in building tools that make work feel human again."
- Good: [delete it — "The paragraph works better without it."]

**6. Em dashes as connectors → lexical_structural**
- (Rule: restructure, use a colon or period.)

**7. "That"-relative-clause padding → lexical_structural**
- Bad: "A platform that connects your entire stack."
- Good: "Connects your entire stack."

## Principle table (bad → good, verbatim)

| Principle | Bad | Good |
|---|---|---|
| Lead with the outcome | "Real-time sync across all your devices" | "Pick up where you left off on any device, instantly" |
| One sentence, one idea | Long sentences with multiple clauses joined by commas | "Break them. Short is clear." |
| Concrete beats abstract | "Saves you time" | "Cuts reporting from 2 hours to 15 minutes" |
| Active voice | "Results are tracked automatically" | "Tracks results automatically" |
| Cut what adds nothing | "We believe in building tools that make work feel human again" | "Delete it." |

## Soft tells (frequency-based; not harm alone — matches slopslap's "candidate aid, not authorization")
Transition-word bloat (Furthermore/Moreover/Additionally); "In today's [X]" openers; fake intimacy ("The truth is...", "Here's the thing..."); "Simply"/"Just" minimizers; "Whether you're X or Y"; symmetrical bullets; gerund headline openers ("Introducing...", "Building..."); recap-and-restate.
