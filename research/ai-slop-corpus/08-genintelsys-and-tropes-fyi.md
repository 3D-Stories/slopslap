## 8A — genintelsys.com — "Em Dashes, 'Delve', and the Other Dead Giveaways of AI Writing"

- **Source URL:** https://www.genintelsys.com/blog/em-dashes-ai-tells/
- **Date:** 2026-05-13
- **License:** commercial blog (promotes "Cloak" tool). No reuse license. Fair-use w/ attribution. Flagged.

**Full before→after pairs (few):**
- Rule-of-three triplet → expansion: "Fast, reliable, and affordable" → "fast and reliable, and the price hasn't moved in three years." (→ synthetic cadence / rule-of-three)

**Tell catalog (verbatim clusters):**
- "delve" cluster: delve, navigate/navigating, tapestry, ever-evolving/ever-changing, landscape, realm, journey, showcase (verb), foster/fostering, multifaceted, nuanced, seamless/seamlessly, robust, leverage/leveraging, utilize, furthermore/moreover, in conclusion.
- Hedge cluster: "It's important to note that," "It's worth mentioning," "While this approach has many benefits," "That said," "However, it's worth considering," "It's crucial to remember," "In today's fast-paced world," "At the end of the day."
- Fake balance (→ synthetic cadence): "It's not just X, it's Y," "More than just X," "X is more than X," "On the one hand... on the other hand..." (where the other hand never lands), "While X may seem Y, in reality it's Z."
- Em-dash rule (verbatim): "The model uses three per paragraph. That's the tell. Volume. ... If you have more than one every two paragraphs, you have too many." — supports slopslap treating em-dash *density*, not presence, as the candidate signal.
- Oxford triplet, bullet reflex, uniform paragraph length, sandwich opening, numbered narrative, intro-that-summarizes, "Conclusion" header, CTA fossils.
- Verbatim caution (aligns w/ keystone): "Some of these patterns are fine in moderation. The volume is what makes a draft read as AI."

## 8B — tropes.fyi — "AI Writing Pattern Directory" (Ossama Hassanein)

- **Source URL:** https://tropes.fyi/  (downloadable `tropes.md`)
- **Date:** (site, active 2026)
- **License:** none stated on page; `tropes.md` is offered to "Copy or download" for system-prompt use, but no explicit OSS license. deslop repo adapts it (MIT) with credit — so effectively re-used downstream, but treat the primary as unclear. Flagged.
- **Structure:** categories — Word Choice[4], Sentence Structure[8], Paragraph Structure[2], Tone[9], Formatting[3], Composition[7]. Each trope named + AI-example snippet (no "after"; it's a name-and-shame directory).

**Named tropes with AI-example snippets (verbatim):**
- **Negative Parallelism** — "It's not bold. It's backwards." (called "the single most commonly identified AI writing tell"). Variants documented: causal ("not because X, but because Y"), em-dash dismissal ("X — not Y"), cross-sentence reframe ("The question isn't X. The question is Y."). More examples: "Feeding isn't nutrition. It's dialysis." / "Half the bugs you chase aren't in your code. They're in your head."
- **"Not X. Not Y. Just Z."** — "Not a bug. Not a feature. A fundamental design flaw."
- **"The X? A Y."** — "The result? Devastating."
- **The "Serves As" Dodge** (→ copula avoidance) — "The building serves as a reminder of the city's heritage."
- **Invented Concept Labels** — "the supervision paradox" (→ epistemic_distortion; note deslop Example 14 "calibration paradox" is the same trope).

**"Deslopify" demo (a partial diff, general/tech register):**
- Before fragment: "The technology is extraordinary, the demos are dazzling, and the marketing is relentless. Yet when you look at how people actually work — the real workflows, the real tools, the real daily grind — AI sits mostly on the sidelines."
- Suggested edits shown: "extraordinary → impressive," "relentless → aggressive," collapse the rule-of-three "the real workflows, the real tools, the real daily grind" → "their actual day-to-day." (→ rule-of-three + em-dash aside)

**Verbatim caution (aligns w/ keystone, strongly):** "One in a piece can be effective; ten in a blog post is a genuine insult to the reader." and "(not every emdash is AI I carefully selected this one)".
