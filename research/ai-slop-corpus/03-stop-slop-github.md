## Before → After pairs (verbatim, genre: business/blog prose)

**1. Throat-clearing + binary contrast → emptiness + synthetic cadence**
- Before: "Here's the thing: building products is hard. Not because the technology is complex. Because people are complex. Let that sink in."
- After: "Building products is hard. Technology is manageable. People aren't."

**2. Filler + unnecessary reassurance → emptiness**
- Before: "It turns out that most teams struggle with alignment. The uncomfortable truth is that nobody wants to admit they're confused. And that's okay."
- After: "Teams struggle with alignment. Nobody admits confusion."

**3. Business-jargon stack → emptiness/promotional**
- Before: "In today's fast-paced landscape, we need to lean into discomfort and navigate uncertainty with clarity. This matters because your competition isn't waiting."
- After: "Move faster. Your competition is."

**4. Dramatic fragmentation → synthetic cadence (rule-of-three + staccato)**
- Before: "Speed. Quality. Cost. You can only pick two. That's it. That's the tradeoff."
- After: "Speed, quality, cost—pick two."  *(note: the "after" keeps an em dash — even this aggressive tool ships one)*

**5. Rhetorical setup → emptiness (signposting)**
- Before: "What if I told you that the best teams don't optimize for productivity? Here's what I mean: they optimize for learning. Think about it."
- After: "The best teams optimize for learning, not productivity."

## Structural pattern catalog (patterns → fix; not full prose pairs but high-value tell taxonomy)

**Binary contrasts (→ slopslap synthetic cadence "X, not Y"):** "Not because X. Because Y." · "[X] isn't the problem. [Y] is." · "The answer isn't X. It's Y." · "It feels like X. It's actually Y." · "The question isn't X. It's Y." · "stops being X and starts being Y" · "not just X but also Y". **Fix: State Y directly. Drop the negation.**

**Negative listing:** "Not a X... Not a Y... A Z." · "It wasn't X. It wasn't Y. It was Z." **Fix: State Z.**

**Dramatic fragmentation:** "[Noun]. That's it. That's the [thing]." · "X. And Y. And Z." · "This unlocks something. [Word]."

**Rhetorical setups:** "What if [reframe]?" · "Here's what I mean:" · "Think about it:" · "And that's okay."

**False agency (→ slopslap anthropomorphic flourish / obscured responsibility):** "a complaint becomes a fix" · "a bet lives or dies in days" · "the decision emerges" · "the culture shifts" · "the conversation moves toward" · "the data tells us" · "the market rewards". **Fix: Name the human. "The team fixed it that week" beats "the complaint becomes a fix."**

**Narrator-from-a-distance:** "Nobody designed this." · "This happens because..." · "This is why..." · "People tend to..."

**Passive voice → obscured responsibility:** "X was created" → name who; "It is believed that" → name who believes; "Mistakes were made" → name who.

**Rhythm patterns:** three-item lists → use two or one; questions answered immediately → let them breathe or cut; em-dashes → remove; staccato fragmentation → don't stack short punchy sentences; "Not always. Not perfectly." (hedging-as-reassurance).

**Word patterns:** lazy extremes (every/always/never/everyone/nobody) → false authority; all adverbs (-ly, really/just/literally/genuinely/honestly/simply/actually) → empty emphasis.
