Design v2 · the pivot RATIFIED by owner 2026-07-13 · base v0.2.2

Find everything.
You hold the pen.

slopslap detects every tell, recommends by genre, and hands you the review — apply, hand-edit, or discard every finding. Then it learns from your clicks.

"Better than humanizer" = do what humanizer does, but more — with a byte-exact guarantee no fact, requirement, or negation breaks, and a user gate no humanizer offers.

the keystone, marked

◦ before — v0.2 (the timid thesis)

Edit authorization comes only from demonstrated editorial harm; the scanner, genre, ratings, and voiceprint never authorize an edit.

◦ after — keystone v2 (proposed)

Every tell is detected and prepared for removal; genre + learned feedback set each finding's recommendation; the user's review decision — not the scanner, not the genre, not the learning — authorizes the edit; and the byte-exact verifier guarantees no applied edit changes a number, requirement, negation, condition, or protected span. Recommendations may learn; authorization never does. ✓

01 The loop

Five stages. You are stage three.

Genre no longer suppresses detection — it gates the recommendation. Nothing applies unseen; nothing unsafe applies at all.

→ 1 detect

Everything

Every tell, every genre — cadence, filler, generic diction. Each finding ships strip-ready with a verifier precheck.

→ 2 recommend

By genre + learning

strip / keep + rationale per finding. Advisory metadata — it never limits what you can do.

→ 3 · you

Review

Interactive doc: apply / edit / discard each finding — hand-tune any proposal, override any recommendation.

→ 4 · gate

Apply

Approved hunks only, through the unchanged 3-layer verifier + backup-first atomic engine.

→ 5 learn

Feedback

Your clicks are labeled data — false positives + genre corrections tune future recommendations.

04 The learning loop

  
Your clicks are the calibration corpus.

  
The #25 calibration harness shipped starved — 0 labeled points (the corpus had no licensed verbatim text). Review decisions manufacture exactly what it lacked.

  

    
What feeds back
Every decision appends to a local feedback ledger: finding, metric, genre, recommendation, your action (+ your replacement on an edit), your reason (false_positive · keep_voice · genre_wrong). False-positive marks raise that metric's threshold in that genre; repeated strip-overrides flip a genre's default. Edits are the richest labels — keeping "innovative" out of an adjective pile says that word was a real claim there, not filler: a partial-accept no binary click can express.

    
What never learns
Authorization. Learning tunes recommendations only. The user gate stays final forever; the verifier stays the hard gate forever — even a user-approved edit that would break a number is blocked and surfaced. Ledger is local, hashed spans only, purgeable.

06 The trade, stated

  
What the verifier can't judge, you now do.

  

    
Residual risk
The verifier guarantees invariants, not taste — an invariant-clean rewrite can still flatten a nuance. v1's answer was timidity.

    
v2's answer
Nothing applies unseen: every rewrite crosses your review with its evidence and rationale. The human gate replaces the timid gate — strictly better on both axes.

  

  

    the product
    
Strip like a humanizer. Guarantee like a spec tool. Decide like an editor. And get better every time you click.

    
Detection universal · recommendation genre-gated + learned · authorization user-only · safety verifier-owned. Four powers, four different owners — that separation is the design.
